Work / 013

Las Caminantes

2024  /  Living installation — weeds, plants, Morse code, speaker  /  Variable dimensions

[ Pending: Las Caminantes — Garden of weeds growing inside exhibition hall ]
[ Pending: Las Caminantes — Detail of plants and speaker emitting Morse code ]
[ Pending: Las Caminantes — Installation view at MAMU ]

A garden of weeds growing inside the exhibition hall. The plants emit via Morse code a message: "In each inhalation we meet." The work transforms the museum space into a living ecosystem where the boundary between the cultivated and the wild, the institutional and the organic, is deliberately dissolved.

The title — Las Caminantes (The Walkers) — refers to the plants themselves, beings that move and migrate despite our perception of them as stationary. Weeds are plants that appear uninvited, that cross boundaries, that resist eradication — qualities that make them both resilient and marginal. By giving them a place inside the museum, Escallon elevates these overlooked organisms to the status of protagonists.

The Morse code message — "In each inhalation we meet" — refers to the fundamental biological exchange between humans and plants: we breathe what they exhale, and they absorb what we release. This invisible reciprocity, occurring with every breath, is the most intimate and constant encounter between species. The coded transmission makes this invisible exchange audible, translating a biological fact into a poetic declaration.

CONTEXT

Presented at MAMU in 2024, Las Caminantes continues Escallon's exploration of the vegetal world begun in Nuevas Floras (2003) and developed in Encuentros con Seres Notables (2022). While those earlier works focused on the morphological and material kinship between humans and trees, here the relationship becomes atmospheric and respiratory — a shared chemistry that sustains both forms of life.
