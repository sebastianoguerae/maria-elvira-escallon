Work / 010

Precipitacion de arenas

2022-2024 (various versions)  /  Installation — sand, furniture, security glass, speaker  /  Variable dimensions

VIDEO

Polvo eres — Related video · 2:00 min

The work seeks to transform a non-physical phenomenon — the passage of time — into something material and tangible. Escallon defines these pieces as "dispositivos para ver pasar el tiempo" [devices for watching time pass]. Golden sand from the Guamo river falls from the ceiling at different intervals, covering surfaces and constantly transforming the state of the room.

Compressed domestic furniture is gradually buried under tons of sand. The space is separated from the viewer by security glass, and a speaker allows one to hear the sound of the falling sand. Personal objects become archaeological remains. In the MAMU version, 10 tons of sand were precipitated for the first time, amplifying the scale and impact of the installation.

The work developed from the earlier concept "Precipitaciones del rio Cauca" [Precipitations of the Cauca River].

CONTEXT

Precipitacion de arenas has had multiple versions, each adapted to the specific exhibition space. The version presented at MAMU in 2024 was the most ambitious, with 10 tons of sand precipitating over the compressed furniture. The piece transforms the exhibition room into a poetic device where time becomes visible through material accumulation.

EXHIBITIONS

  • Encuentros en la fabrica de polvo — MAMU, Bogota, 2024. Most ambitious version (10 tons of sand).
  • Galeria Espacio Continuo — Bogota, 2022.
