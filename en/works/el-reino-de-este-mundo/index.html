Work / 002

El Reino de Este Mundo

2000  /  Installation — concrete blocks, debris  /  Variable dimensions

A wall built with concrete blocks agglomerating debris from a demolished family house. The wall projects a 'shadow' formed with scattered debris on the museum floor. The installation transforms domestic destruction into monumental presence, converting the remnants of private life into a public architectural gesture.

The title references Alejo Carpentier's novel The Kingdom of This World (1949), establishing a dialogue between the literary notion of the marvelous real and the material reality of destruction. The debris — fragments of tiles, plaster, domestic materials — carry the memory of the house they once formed, now reorganized into a new structure that simultaneously commemorates and displaces the original.

The 'shadow' cast by the wall onto the floor operates as a metaphor for absence: the debris that does not make it into the consolidated structure spreads across the ground, suggesting the impossibility of fully containing the remains of what has been lost. The work oscillates between construction and ruin, between monument and residue.

CONTEXT

Presented at the Museo de Arte Moderno de Bogota (MAMBO), El Reino de Este Mundo marks an early instance of Escallon's engagement with architecture, demolition, and the material traces of lived experience. The work anticipates themes that would recur throughout her practice — the transformation of domestic space, the tension between presence and absence, and the use of debris as both material and meaning.
