Nuevas Floras Versalles

2017  /  Sculptural intervention on trees  /  Arboretum de Chevreloup & Jardin des Tuileries, France

In 2017, Maria Elvira Escallon brought Nuevas Floras to France, intervening on trees at the Arboretum de Chevreloup and presenting the work at the Jardin des Tuileries during FIAC (Foire Internationale d'Art Contemporain) in Paris.

This version responds to the specificities of the French landscape and history — a dialogue between the carved baroque forms and the formal gardens of the European landscape tradition. The Arboretum de Chevreloup, with its collection of more than 2,500 tree species, offers a context in which Escallon's intervention enters into direct conversation with centuries of human manipulation of nature.

Unlike the Colombian version, where the carvings dialogue with colonial baroque, in Versailles the work is situated at the heart of the tradition that originated that same ornamental repertoire — closing a cycle between the European source and its reinterpretation in the Latin American context.
