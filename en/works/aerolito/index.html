Pequeno Museo del Aerolito de Santa Rosa de Viterbo

2014-2015  /  Research, installation, historical and photographic archives, iron replica  /  Variable dimensions

VIDEO

Meteorito — Pequeno Museo del Aerolito · Documentary video · 2:00 min

Comprising various departments, collections, historical and photographic archives that track the history of the meteorite that fell in the department of Boyaca on a Holy Saturday in 1810. The project includes an iron replica of the aerolite currently held by the Museo Nacional.

The installation realized for "Encuentros en la fabrica de polvo" [Encounters in the Dust Factory] at MAMU (2024) was the most complete ever produced, installed in the El Parqueadero hall. The Pequeno Museo functions as an institution within an institution — a fictional museum that questions the ways in which knowledge is constructed, preserved, and exhibited.

Each "department" of the museum addresses a different aspect: geological history, cultural impact, local narratives, and scientific documentation.

CONTEXT

The meteorite fell in Santa Rosa de Viterbo, Boyaca, on a Holy Saturday in 1810 — the same year as Colombia's independence movement. This temporal coincidence adds layers of meaning about origins, cosmic events, and national identity.

The original aerolite rests in the Museo Nacional de Colombia and has been the subject of multiple narratives: scientific, religious, popular, and institutional. Escallon builds a museographic device that brings them all together, questioning the authority of each and revealing how knowledge is fabricated as much as it is discovered.
