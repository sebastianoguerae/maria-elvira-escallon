VIDEO

Polvo eres

Project planned for the central hall of the Hospital Santa Rosa. Stop-motion video of the phrase "Polvo eres" [Dust thou art] slowly appearing as it is carved with chisel and mallet on the reception wall of the hall. The blows appear on the wall; the subject performing the action is never visible. It was first projected on a wall of the Hospital Santa Rosa in 2008 and later carved into the wall of the Art Museum of the Universidad Nacional de Colombia.

Subsequently, as time has passed, this gesture has been repeated, taking on other connotations in other spaces. "Polvo eres" as stardust, in the sense that we are made of the same atoms as the stars. "Polvo eres" in the sense that we are not as solid as we perceive ourselves to be; we are really clusters of molecules, and we are dissolving to the extent that "everything is burning," as the Buddha says.
