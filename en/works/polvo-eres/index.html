Work / 012

Polvo Eres

2024  /  Wall carving — brick, plaster, paint  /  ~60 x 400 cm

[ Pending: Polvo Eres — Detail of carved lettering and debris at floor level ]
[ Pending: Polvo Eres — Installation view at MAMU ]

Words carved directly into a museum wall, exposing internal layers — beneath white paint, the brick layer is revealed — while debris accumulates at floor level. The biblical phrase "Thou art dust and unto dust thou shalt return" is inscribed not on the wall's surface but into its body, requiring the destruction of the wall itself to make the text legible.

The act of carving reverses the usual relationship between writing and its support. Rather than adding material (ink, paint) to a surface, Escallon removes material, creating text through subtraction. The words emerge from the wall's own substance — plaster, brick, the accumulated layers of institutional architecture — making the medium and the message inseparable.

The debris that falls to the floor as the carving proceeds becomes part of the work: a material enactment of the very phrase being inscribed. The dust from which we come and to which we return is not merely invoked but produced, accumulating beneath the text as its inevitable byproduct. The wall itself begins its return to dust in the act of speaking about it.

CONTEXT

Presented at MAMU in 2024, Polvo Eres is a site-specific intervention that will be destroyed when the exhibition ends and the wall is repaired or demolished. This built-in impermanence reinforces the work's meditation on mortality and the transience of all material forms — including the museum wall that typically serves as a neutral, permanent backdrop for art.

The work connects to Escallon's broader investigation of institutional architecture as material rather than mere container, and to her sustained engagement with processes of decay, erosion, and return to elemental states — themes present across works from In Memoriam to Precipitacion de Arenas.
