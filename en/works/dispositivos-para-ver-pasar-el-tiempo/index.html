Dispositivos para Ver Pasar el Tiempo

2020  /  Installation, sculpture  /  Variable dimensions

Is it possible to create a device to see time pass? This question articulates a series of pieces that seek to make the intangible tangible. The work proposes objects and mechanisms through which the passage of time — normally invisible, perceivable only through its effects — becomes something one can witness directly.

Each device in the series approaches the problem from a different angle, using material processes — erosion, accumulation, dissolution, transformation — as instruments for rendering time visible. The pieces function not as clocks that measure time abstractly but as apparatuses that embody it materially, making duration something experienced rather than counted.

The title itself — Devices for Watching Time Pass — frames the work as both poetic proposition and technical challenge. Escallon's devices are not scientific instruments but sculptural ones, and their effectiveness lies not in precision but in presence: they ask the viewer to pause and witness processes that unfold at a pace radically different from the accelerated rhythms of contemporary life.

CONTEXT

Created in 2020, during a year of global pause and forced stillness, the work resonates with a collective experience of time distorted — stretched, compressed, made strange. The series extends Escallon's long engagement with temporality, connecting to earlier works like In Memoriam (ice dissolving over 20 hours) and anticipating Precipitacion de Arenas (sand accumulating over the duration of an exhibition).
