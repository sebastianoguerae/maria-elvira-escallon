Work / 008

En el fertil suelo — La travesia

2017–2018  /  Video, sculpture  /  Variable dimensions

Inspired by Homer's Iliad — specifically the passage in which Achaeans and Trojans, after ten years of war, decide to lay down their arms on the battlefield — the work presents two characters who, despite enormous impediments, have found each other in order to move forward.

The artist perceives them as "antiguos adversarios que regresan de la guerra y emprenden un viaje que con frecuencia se lleva a cabo en soledad y hacia la incertidumbre." The work relates this historical moment of vulnerability between former enemies to the contemporary sociopolitical context of Colombia and its peace process.

The project includes light and sound installations that explore "la enredada administracion de justicia en la Colombia contemporanea," using sensory elements — sounds, shadows, footsteps — rather than directly representing the protagonists.

CONTEXT

Created during the period following Colombia's peace agreement (signed in 2016), the work addresses the difficult transition from conflict to coexistence without being literal. The Homeric reference allows Escallon to speak about the present through antiquity, finding in the Greek epic a mirror for the Colombian moment.

The title itself — En el fertil suelo — evokes both Colombian soil and the battlefield where arms are laid down, establishing a tension between fertility and destruction, between what grows and what has been devastated.
