Work / 023

Precipitaciones de arenas del rio Cauca

2008–2009  /  Installation — river sand, furniture  /  41st National Artists' Salon, Cali

Sand from the Cauca River was continuously poured into a room at the Colegio Sagrada Familia in Cali. The space contained waiting-room furniture — chairs, tables — that was gradually buried under the sand as the exhibition progressed. The process was slow, visible only to those who returned to the room at different moments.

The work operates as a temporal device: it makes the passage of time visible through material accumulation. The waiting-room furniture — objects designed for transit, for the pause before something that will come — become buried, fossilized objects. Waiting is transformed into burial. What was meant to be provisional becomes permanent.

The Cauca River, which runs through the Valle del Cauca and whose sands have been witnesses and repositories of Colombian violence, adds an additional layer of meaning. The sand is not a neutral material: it carries the history of the territory with it. This work constituted the conceptual origin of what would later develop into Precipitacion de arenas.

CONTEXT

The installation was presented at the 41st National Artists' Salon in Cali, curated by Jaime Ceron. Maria Elvira Escallon participated by invitation of the artist Wilson Diaz. The Colegio Sagrada Familia, as the venue for the work, added an institutional resonance — a space of education and formation turned into a scene of accumulation and burial.
