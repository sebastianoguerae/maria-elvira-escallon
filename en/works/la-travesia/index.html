Work / 022

La travesia

2017–2024  /  Installation — used and discarded wooden doors, mirror  /  2.40 x 15 x 1.80 m

La travesia [The Crossing] is a corridor built with wooden doors salvaged from demolished family homes. The used and discarded doors preserve the marks of time and domestic use — handprints, replaced locks, layers of paint. Arranged one after another, they form a passage that the viewer can physically walk through.

At the end of the corridor, a mirror produces an infinite reflection of these thresholds, multiplying the succession of doors until they vanish from sight. The effect transforms the finite walk into an endless crossing — a metaphor for the transits, relocations, and displacements that these doors once mediated between the public and the private, between the inside and the outside.

The door, as a threshold, condenses the history of those who passed through it. Each of these doors was the boundary of a home that no longer exists. Gathered together, they constitute a material archive of domestic lives erased by demolition and urban development. The mirror, by multiplying them, suggests that this loss is immeasurable.

CONTEXT

The work was exhibited at MAMU (Museo de Arte Miguel Urrutia) in 2024, within the "Ante el tiempo" [Before Time] section of the exhibition Encuentros en la fabrica de polvo [Encounters in the Dust Factory]. In this context, La travesia dialogues with other works by Escallon that address heritage, material memory, and the tension between permanence and disappearance.
