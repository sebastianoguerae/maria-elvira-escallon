Nuevas Floras do Sul

2011  /  Sculptural intervention on trees  /  Jesuit ruins, southern Brazil

Nuevas Floras do Sul is the version of Nuevas Floras realized in southern Brazil, in the vicinity of the Jesuit mission ruins. Escallon intervenes living trees with Baroque-form carvings — the same procedure originally developed in Colombia in 2003 — but here the geographic and historical context adds a particular dimension.

The Baroque carvings on the trees enter into direct dialogue with the historical Baroque of the ruined Jesuit missions. The Jesuit reductions of southern Brazil, founded in the seventeenth and eighteenth centuries, were centers of artistic production where European Baroque ornamentation merged with indigenous Guarani traditions. The ruins of those missions preserve fragments of that cultural hybridization.

By carving Baroque forms onto living trees alongside those ruins, Escallon produces a temporal echo: the Baroque is once again inscribed upon nature, but this time in an artistic gesture conscious of the history that precedes it. The trees, as in all versions of Nuevas Floras, will over time respond by absorbing and transforming the intervention — making visible the relationship between culture and nature that the Jesuit missions also embodied in their own way.
