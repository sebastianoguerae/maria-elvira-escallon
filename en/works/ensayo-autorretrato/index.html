Ensayo Autorretrato

2022  /  Video  /  Espacio Continuo

[ Pending: Ensayo Autorretrato — Video still, hands searching a wall ]
[ Pending: Ensayo Autorretrato — Video still, hands destroying Venus de Milo mask ]
[ Pending: Ensayo Autorretrato — Video still, arm modeled connecting to frailejon ]

In video, the artist's hands search a wall, scratch with their nails, find a nail. In another sequence, her hands systematically destroy a Venus de Milo mask, then model an arm connecting not to a shoulder but to a frailejon — a plant of the Colombian paramos. The self-portrait is constructed not through the face but through the hands — the instruments of the artist's practice.

The title — Ensayo Autorretrato (Self-Portrait Essay) — signals both an attempt and a literary form. This is not a definitive self-portrait but an essay, a trial, an exploration. The video unfolds as a series of gestures that reveal the artist's relationship to materials, surfaces, and the act of making itself. To scratch, to find, to destroy, to model — these are the verbs of Escallon's practice, distilled into their most elemental form.

The destruction of the Venus de Milo mask — an icon of classical Western beauty — and the subsequent modeling of an arm that connects to a frailejon enact a symbolic displacement: from European canonical beauty to Colombian highland ecology, from the idealized human form to the hybrid human-vegetal body. The frailejon, a plant endemic to the paramos of the Andes, becomes a surrogate for the shoulder, suggesting that the artist's body extends into and is completed by the landscape she inhabits.

CONTEXT

Presented at Espacio Continuo in 2022, Ensayo Autorretrato is one of Escallon's most explicitly personal works, yet it avoids conventional self-portraiture entirely. The face never appears; identity is conveyed through action rather than image. The work connects to Encuentros con Seres Notables (also 2022) in its exploration of human-vegetal kinship, and to the broader arc of Escallon's practice in its engagement with destruction as a creative act.
