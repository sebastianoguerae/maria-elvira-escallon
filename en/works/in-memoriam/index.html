Work / 003

In Memoriam

2001  /  Ephemeral sculpture — ice, glass, water  /  300 kg, 20 hours

[ Pending: In Memoriam — Ice column at full height inside glass container ]
[ Pending: In Memoriam — Column mid-dissolution, water rising ]
[ Pending: In Memoriam — Final state, water and remnants ]

A 300-kilogram ice column dissolving slowly in water over twenty hours inside a glass container. The work captures the passage of time as irreversible material loss. As the ice melts, the solid mass transforms into the very liquid that surrounds and eventually replaces it — a process that is at once predictable and deeply affecting.

In Memoriam operates as a meditation on impermanence and the impossibility of preservation. The glass container makes the process entirely visible — the viewer witnesses a slow disappearance in real time. The title invokes the language of commemoration, yet the work itself enacts the opposite: not the fixing of memory but its dissolution.

The ephemeral nature of the sculpture — existing fully only at the moment of its inauguration and progressively ceasing to exist — places temporality at the center of the work. What remains at the end is not the sculpture but its trace: water, the memory of a form, and the experience of having witnessed its passing.

CONTEXT

In Memoriam belongs to a lineage of works in which Escallon engages with materials undergoing transformation — processes of decay, dissolution, and disappearance that would become central to her practice. The work anticipates later explorations of time-based material processes, such as Precipitacion de Arenas and Dispositivos para Ver Pasar el Tiempo.
