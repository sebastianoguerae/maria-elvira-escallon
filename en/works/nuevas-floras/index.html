Nuevas Floras — Temporal Transformation

In Nuevas Floras the artist intervenes on living native trees with carvings or prostheses of forms generally evocative of classical architecture and common elements in baroque church altars. Based on prior research, Escallon determined how to proceed without causing collateral damage, and the carved fragments were executed by experts.

In a second moment, the arboreal beings respond to the action by interrupting the intervention, until making it almost imperceptible. Her practice creates a space where the 'wild' and the managed converge in time, where nature and culture are inextricably bound. The artist continues photographing these trees as they grow, making them active participants in the work.

The series presents as large-format chromogenic photographs (120 x 80 cm) taken immediately after completing the intervention. Since 2004, the project became a nomadic proposal responding to the specificities of culture-nature relationships at each site, with versions carried out in Colombia, the United Kingdom (Bristol), and France (Versailles and Chevreloup).

Curatorial text — Jose Roca

Jose Roca — Columna de Arena 53, August 2003 Escallon's work aims to disturb the naturalness of that order. Nuevas Floras possesses two fundamental aspects: its nomadic character — because the idea from which it departs can be developed in other spaces — and the concept of temporality — because its duration is indefinite.

CONTEXT

In 2003, Escallon won the First Prize of the III Luis Caballero Competition with this project, presented at the Galeria Santa Fe in Bogota. The jury, composed of curator Jose Roca among others, highlighted the way in which the work questions the boundaries between the natural and the cultural — a constant in the artist's trajectory.

The carvings made on the trees evoke forms from the Colombian colonial ornamental repertoire — Solomonic columns, volutes, elements of baroque altarpieces — creating a tension between the imposed cultural history and the organic response of the living being that absorbs and transforms it. Over the years, the bark reclaims the carvings, deforms them, integrates them, or makes them disappear.
